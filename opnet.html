1. Routing algorithms in adhoc networks

At INRIA (Institut National de Recherche en Informatique et Automatique  ) in the HiPERCOM (High PERformance COMmunication) group, we are designing routing algorithms for ad-hoc networks. HiPERCOM group is working at IETF in the MANET (Mobile Adhoc Networks) workgroup where it is proposing the OLSR (Optimized Link State Routing) protocol. 

To support this action we have several projects. Among them is the RNRT  PRIMA project. The main goal of PRIMA was to study the use of routing protocol in wireless ad-hoc network.  Applications were envisionned in rescue and aeronautical  networks.

In the PRIMA project, the OPNET tool was used to study two routing protocols.

  •      INRIA in charge of proactive routing protocols has extensiveley studied the OLSR protocol,
  •      EADS Sycomore in charge of reactive routing protocols has extensively studied the AODV protocol.
2. Results

Significant parts of the results obtained throughout the PRIMA project are published in the following publically available documents. 

     " An efficient Simulation Model for Wireless LANs Applied to the IEEE 802.11 Standard ". Paul Mühlethaler, Abdellah Najid, to appear in  RSRCP 
     also in INRIA RR-4182, April 2001.

     "Simulation Results of the OLSR Routing Protocol for Wireless Network".Laouti, Anis - Mühlethaler, Paul - Najid, Abdellah - Plakoo, Epiphane 
     Plakoo. Mediterranean Ad Hoc Networking Workshop, Med-hoc-Net 2002. Sardaigna 4- 6 September 2002. also in the INRIA RR-4414 March 
     2002.

      «Unicast et Multicast dans les réseaux adhoc sans fil» Anis Laouiti. Phd Thesis. University  Versailles Saint Quentin. July 2002.

These documents specifically refer to simulation results obtained with  OPNET.

To briefly summarize the obtained results, we have validated the OLSR protocol for various scenarios corresponding to specific applications. We have also carefully investigated the interaction beween the  IEEE 802.11 MAC layer and the routing layer.  We also  have  proposed a very efficient model for IEEE 802.11.
